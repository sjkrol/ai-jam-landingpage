## Overview

AI Design Jam 2026 is a design competition and research initiative examining how artificial intelligence is being integrated into contemporary architectural workflows.

The competition invites students and practitioners to design a small pavilion responding to the theme of *Iteration* — and to document, openly and honestly, how AI-enabled tools shaped that process.

The theme is Iteration — a word that belongs equally to design culture and to the AI tools reshaping it.

The pavilion is the design challenge. The process documentation is the research contribution. Both matter.

Entries will be evaluated by an expert jury. Selected works will be exhibited publicly and contribute to an anonymised research analysis of emerging AI integration strategies in design.

This is not a competition about having the most sophisticated AI workflow. It is about using new tools to produce good design.


## Theme: Iteration

Iteration is fundamental to design thinking — the cycle of proposition, evaluation, and refinement through which ideas develop. It is also native to the way AI tools operate: generating outputs, receiving feedback, and producing successive variations in response.

This competition invites participants to design a pavilion in which the iterative nature of the design process is legible — not just in the final form, but in the decisions that shaped it. Proposals may explore iteration at any scale: in the development of a structural system, the refinement of a spatial sequence, the negotiation between human judgement and computational output, or the relationship between the pavilion and its landscape setting.

There is no prescribed level of AI sophistication. A single tool used with clarity and intentionality is as valid as a complex multi-model workflow. What the jury will look for is evidence of a design process that moved — that changed, responded, and arrived somewhere through iteration rather than in spite of it.


## The Challenge

Design a pavilion for Birrarung Marr — on the Yarra River, with Melbourne as its backdrop — subject to site confirmation.

The pavilion should be capable of hosting 50 people comfortably for a seated event such as a talk or forum, and 150 people as a relaxed crowd for a social or cultural gathering.

AI-enabled tools must be integrated into the design workflow. These may include text-to-image diffusion models, large language models, generative scripting tools, parametric systems, or custom and hybrid workflows.

The emphasis is not on spectacle, but on the clarity and intentionality of the design process.

## Eligibility

Submissions must meet the following requirements to be considered for evaluation:

- Completed by an individual or team currently enrolled in an architecture or related discipline, or working in architectural practice
- Designed using at least one AI-enabled tool as an integral part of the workflow
- All AI tools used in the design process must be disclosed in the process documentation deck
- Submitted as a single A1 presentation panel and a process documentation deck of up to 10 slides
- Original work produced for this competition — not previously published or submitted elsewhere
- Submitted in digital format by the closing date


## Deliverables

### 1. Presentation Panel

1 × A1 panel, portrait orientation, submitted in digital format (PDF, 150dpi minimum).

The panel should include:

- A hero image of the pavilion
- A narrative diagram representing the design
- A design statement (max 200 words)

### 2. Process Documentation Deck

Up to 10 × A4 slides documenting the main stages of the AI-assisted design workflow.

Each stage should be structured as:

*Inputs → Model → Output*

Inputs may include sketches, photographs, reference images, text prompts, geometry, or any other material fed into the model. For each stage, participants should identify the tool type and model name where known. Outputs should be shown as produced — before any manual editing or curation.

Where a stage involved human intervention between AI outputs — sketching, modelling, editing, scripting — this should be noted briefly.

Participants determine what constitutes a main stage. There is no minimum number of stages required.

## Evaluation Criteria

Entries will be evaluated by a jury of three experts. Jury composition will be announced following the submission deadline.

| Criteria | Weight |
|---|---|
| Architectural Quality + Response to Theme + Design Narrative | 60% |
| AI Workflow Documentation + Presentation & Clarity | 40% |

**Architectural Quality** — Spatial coherence, structural logic, contextual sensitivity, and the overall resolution of the design proposal.

**Response to Theme** — The degree to which the proposal engages meaningfully with the theme of Iteration — formally, spatially, or conceptually.

**Design Narrative** — The clarity and conviction of the story told about the design — how decisions were made, what was rejected, and why the proposal arrived where it did.

**AI Workflow Documentation** — The transparency and intentionality of the documented AI-assisted process. There is no penalty for simple workflows — clarity of use is valued over sophistication.

**Presentation & Clarity** — The legibility and quality of the submitted materials.

## Why Participate?

Architecture is navigating a technological transition that will reshape how we design, document, and deliver buildings. AI tools are already present in practice and education — but their affordances for spatial thinking, design development, and human-centred outcomes remain largely unexplored.

This competition is an opportunity to test those affordances seriously — as a design challenge with real constraints, real evaluation, and a public outcome.

Whether you are a student finding your footing with new tools, or a practitioner rethinking established workflows, this is a structured space to engage with questions the profession needs to answer.
